My body let me down." • Hook: In the mirror, I wore the grief—and still, I carried you. --- **Style Descriptions** **200 Character Style** Soft, intimate folk ballad with spare phrasing and gentle slant rhyme. Rooted in Rebecca’s own words: “completely floored,” “hated my body,” “no symptoms,” “held her.” Tone is grounded, compassionate, trauma-informed. **300 Character Style** An emotive acoustic-folk song with quiet guitar, narrow octave melody, and natural phrasing that reflects Rebecca Adlington’s candor: she “could not stop crying for three days,” felt guilt, her body betrayed her—yet they “held her” baby Harper. No invented scenes, only deep honesty. --- **Song Description** This narrative-style lyric captures Rebecca Adlington’s raw, public recounting of her 20-week miscarriage in 2023: the emotional breakdown, self-blame, body-image struggle, and the profound moment of holding her daughter, Harper. All lines trace to her direct quotes—whether in _This Morning_ segment (August 2025) or Instagram posts—and are framed without embellishment or speculation. Dr. Zoe Williams’s perspective adds context around silence and the importance of openness. --- **Lyrics** [Verse 1] We went to the scan, expecting hope— and then no heartbeat, no answer why. “I couldn’t stop crying for three days,” my tears spoke what my voice could not say. [Pre-Chorus] I followed every rule, blamed myself— “I was angry at my body. My body let me down.” [Chorus / Hook] In the mirror, I wore the grief— still 20-week swollen, still carrying you. [Outro] We carry you, Harper, in the ache and the hush— your absence traced in breath, in love.
